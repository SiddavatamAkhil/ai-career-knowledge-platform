Aurora Robotics — AR-200 Warehouse Robot Technical Overview

1. Hardware Specifications
The AR-200 unit weighs 42 kg and has a maximum payload capacity of 60 kg.
Battery life is rated at 10 hours of continuous operation on a single charge,
with a fast-charge option that restores 80% capacity in 45 minutes. The unit
navigates using a combination of LiDAR, depth cameras, and wheel odometry.

2. Software Architecture
The AR-200 runs a ROS2-based navigation stack. Path planning uses a hybrid
A* algorithm combined with a dynamic window approach for real-time obstacle
avoidance. Fleet coordination across multiple robots is handled by a central
fleet manager service that assigns tasks using a greedy nearest-available-robot
heuristic, with periodic rebalancing every 90 seconds.

3. Safety Features
The AR-200 is certified to ANSI/RIA R15.08 safety standards for mobile
industrial robots. It includes emergency stop buttons on all four sides,
a 360-degree obstacle detection radius of 1.5 meters, and automatic
speed reduction to 0.3 m/s when operating within 2 meters of a detected
human worker.

4. Deployment and Integration
Customers typically deploy an initial pilot fleet of 3-5 units before scaling
to full warehouse coverage. Integration with existing warehouse management
systems (WMS) is done via a REST API and supports SAP EWM, Manhattan Active,
and Blue Yonder out of the box. Average time-to-first-pick after installation
is 2 weeks, including staff training.

5. Maintenance
Scheduled maintenance is recommended every 500 operating hours and includes
wheel inspection, sensor calibration, and battery health diagnostics. The
AR-200's modular design allows most components, including the battery pack
and drive motors, to be swapped in under 15 minutes without specialized tools.

6. Pricing Model
Aurora Robotics offers the AR-200 under a Robot-as-a-Service (RaaS) model,
starting at $2,400 per robot per month, which includes hardware, software
updates, and maintenance. Customers may also purchase units outright for
approximately $85,000 per unit.
